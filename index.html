The Architecture of Indian Democracy

India follows a federal structure with three distinct tiers of governance, ensuring representation from the national capital to the smallest village ward.

Hierarchy of Representation

Tier 1: Union Government (Lok Sabha)
Tier 2: State Governments (Vidhan Sabha)
Tier 3A: Rural (Panchayat)
Tier 3B: Urban (Municipal)
NATIONAL TIER

Sansad Bhavan (Parliament)

Level 01 • Union Governance

Central Election (Lok Sabha)

The Lok Sabha is the House of the People. Citizens directly elect 543 Members of Parliament (MPs) across the country. The party that secures a majority forms the Union Government.

Constituencies 543 Seats
Authority Prime Minister
REGIONAL TIER

Vidhan Sabha (Assembly)

Level 02 • Regional Governance

State Election (Vidhan Sabha)

Each state elects its own Legislative Assembly. MLAs are responsible for state-specific laws and regional development. The leader of the majority party becomes the Chief Minister.

States 28 Assemblies
Authority Chief Minister
RURAL TIER

Gram Panchayat

Level 03A • Grassroots Democracy

Panchayat Elections

The foundation of rural Indian development. Gram Panchayats ensure local governance, agricultural planning, and village infrastructure are managed by the community.

Village Head Sarpanch
Local Rep Ward Member
URBAN TIER

Municipalities & Wards

Level 03B • Urban Democracy

Municipal Elections

Crucial for urban infrastructure. Municipal Corporations manage city planning, waste management, and civic amenities. Councillors represent individual city wards.

City Head Mayor
Local Rep Ward Councillor
EVM & VVPAT Guide

The EVM & VVPAT Guide

Everything you need to know about the Electronic Voting Machine, how it works, and your rights inside the polling station.

01 • Technology

How It Works

An EVM consists of two units: the Control Unit (kept with the Presiding Officer) and the Balloting Unit (placed inside the voting compartment). They are connected by a 5-meter cable.

  • Runs on a single 6-volt alkaline battery (no external power needed).
  • Standalone design: Not connected to the internet or any network.
  • Can record a maximum of 2,000 votes per machine.
02 • Process

Casting Your Vote

Step 1: Setup - The Presiding Officer enables the Ballot Unit after verifying your identity.

Step 2: Cast Vote - Press the blue button next to your chosen candidate's name/symbol.

Step 3: Verify - A slip appears in the VVPAT window for 7 seconds showing your choice.

  1. The Presiding Officer presses "Ballot" on the Control Unit to activate your Balloting Unit.
  2. Enter the voting compartment. A green light will be glowing on the EVM.
  3. Press the blue button next to your chosen candidate's symbol.
  4. A red light will flash next to your choice, followed by a long beep. Look at the VVPAT window for 7 seconds to verify your printed slip!
03 • Rules

Polling Station Protocol

✅ DOs

  • Carry your Voter ID (EPIC) or an approved valid photo ID.
  • Verify your name in the electoral roll beforehand.
  • Look at the VVPAT machine window for 7 seconds to verify your slip.
  • Maintain absolute secrecy of your vote.

❌ DON'Ts

  • Do not carry mobile phones or cameras inside the booth.
  • Do not press multiple buttons at once on the EVM.
  • Do not stay in the voting compartment longer than necessary.
  • Do not ask polling agents who to vote for inside the booth.
